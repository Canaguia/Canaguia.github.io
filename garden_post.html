The Garden: Postmortem

Adventures in the Animation Pipeline

I had the tremendous privelage to be able to work with the kind and talented people at ARL Animation Research Labs where I got to experience working in an animation production pipeline environment alongside an interdisciplinary group of students from several different fields!

I got to work with a team of of 17 dedicated students and teaching assistants for over a year on two major animated film projects. This post is to reflect on my year long exeperience with ARL working on two short-films and multiple side projects and the lessons learned, the challenges faced and how they were overcome.

Planning

Before development on the year long film a pilot film with a smaller team was done. This shortfilm was done over a much smaller timeframe with 6 others. The core of this project was creating a character known as "Greed". Key points at this stage was gathering reference and rapid storyboarding.

2. Development & Implementation

As rigging and scripting lead my contributions were: - Understanding the exsiting capstone auto-rigging shelves built in Maya and Python - Creating files to apply auto-rigging to humanoid and non-humanoid models - Adding to the existing capstone toolset via a neat little script to help animators copy skin-weights within maya to models with the same geometry (was useful as many characters had several duplicated leaf-geometries) 


def copy_skin_weights():
    # Get all selected objects with the specific suffix "_source"
    selection = cmds.ls(sl=True, long=True)
    selection_names = [name.split('|')[-1] for name in selection]
    print(selection_names)
    source_objects = [obj for obj in selection_names if obj.endswith('_source')]
    
    for source_obj in source_objects:
        target_obj = source_obj.replace('_source', '')
        
        if cmds.objExists(target_obj):
            cmds.select([source_obj, target_obj])
            cmds.CopySkinWeights()
        else:
            print("Target object '{}' not found.".format(target_obj))
